Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

About Dr. Kalam

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, also known as the Missile Man of India, was an aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He made significant contributions to India's civilian space program and military missile development. Dr. Kalam was also a teacher, author, and inspirational speaker who touched the lives of millions.

Early Life

Dr. Kalam was born into a humble family and worked hard to support his education. He graduated in physics and aerospace engineering and went on to work with the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

Achievements

  • Missile Man of India: Played a pivotal role in India's missile program (Agni, Prithvi, Akash missiles).
  • Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: Key figure in India's nuclear tests (1998).
  • President of India: 11th President (2002-2007), known for people-centric approach and vision for a developed India.
  • Scientist and Engineer: Renowned aerospace engineer, crucial role in India's Satellite Launch Vehicle project (Rohini satellite, 1980).
  • Author and Motivational Speaker: Prolific writer (e.g., Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds), motivational speaker inspiring students.
  • Education Advocate: Strong advocate for education, particularly for rural youth.
  • Awards and Recognitions:
    Numerous awards, including Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian honor).

Beyond Science

The Paid Grinder

This story showcases Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's honesty and strong principles. Despite being offered a complimentary grinder as a gesture of goodwill, Dr. Kalam declined to accept it without paying. This reflects his belief in fairness and integrity. Later, he took the effort to send the company the exact price of the grinder from his own personal account. This act highlights his commitment to ethical behavior and his insistence on paying for what he receives, regardless of his status or the intentions behind the gift.

Lunch with Children

Dr. Kalam's compassion and empathy are illustrated in this story. During a visit to a school, instead of accepting a lavish lunch prepared by the principal, he chose to share a simple meal with the students, sitting on the floor alongside them. This gesture demonstrated his humility and his ability to connect with young people on their level. It showed that he valued simplicity and the importance of making everyone feel included and respected, regardless of their background or social status.

The Disaster Launch

This story highlights Dr. Kalam's ability to remain calm and composed under pressure, a crucial trait for a leader in aerospace and defense. During a critical launch, there was a leak that could have jeopardized the mission. Demonstrating resourcefulness and quick thinking, Dr. Kalam used a rolled-up newspaper to fix the leak temporarily, allowing the team to successfully reschedule the launch. This incident not only underscores his problem-solving skills and calm demeanor but also his determination to find solutions in the face of unexpected challenges.
